# COLORS OF LIFE - Scene Writing Checklist

## Before Writing Any Scene

### Story Continuity Check
- [ ] What year is this scene? (Verify against timeline in skeleton.md)
- [ ] How old is Vikram in this scene?
- [ ] How old is Adarsh in this scene (if present)?
- [ ] What does Adarsh KNOW at this point? (Critical: He's reconstructing story, can't know what he hasn't learned yet)
- [ ] What does the READER know at this point?
- [ ] Which mysteries must remain hidden in this scene?
- [ ] Is Rehman mentioned/present? (If yes: Must feel REAL, never hint he's imagined until reveal)

### Scene Purpose Check
- [ ] What is the PRIMARY emotional purpose of this scene?
- [ ] What specific character truth does this reveal?
- [ ] What question does this answer from previous scene?
- [ ] What new question does this plant for next scene?
- [ ] Does this scene earn its place or is it just "connecting tissue"?

### Object/Symbol Check
- [ ] What is the ONE memorable object in this scene?
- [ ] Does this object carry emotional weight (not just decoration)?
- [ ] Is the symbolism EARNED through action, not explained?
- [ ] Will readers remember this object 50 pages later?

## During Writing - EVERY Block (50-100 lines)

### Biswa's Voice - THE CRITICAL RULE
- [ ] **Does EVERY sentence or block have a twist/surprise/humor/sadness/suspense?**
- [ ] Am I falling into "essay mode" (explaining feelings instead of showing)?
- [ ] Are there any boring, flat, "connecting" sentences that serve only to move plot?
- [ ] Could ANY sentence be deleted without losing emotional impact?
- [ ] Read aloud: Does it sound like a 72-year-old doctor remembering, or like AI writing?

### Narrative Sequence (From STYLE.md)
- [ ] **Camera first** - What do readers SEE before knowing what it means?
- [ ] **Narrator later** - When does Adarsh's voice enter to guide?
- [ ] **Thoughts last** - When does internal reflection come?
- [ ] **Philosophy earned** - Is any wisdom SHOWN through action before being stated?

### Scene Construction
- [ ] Did I begin INSIDE a living moment (not with explanation)?
- [ ] Am I SHOWING before EXPLAINING?
- [ ] Are there at least 2-3 simultaneous events happening (controlled chaos)?
- [ ] Does the scene have natural rhythm shifts? (Example: humor → tension → compassion)
- [ ] Are there physical details that ground readers in the moment?

### Character Authenticity
- [ ] Does Vikram feel like a REAL PERSON or a character serving plot?
- [ ] Does he have independent goals/reactions beyond the scene's "purpose"?
- [ ] Are supporting characters alive with their own concerns?
- [ ] Would Biswa recognize these people if he met them?

### Dialogue Check
- [ ] Is anyone making a speech? (DELETE - people don't talk in speeches)
- [ ] Are there any "on-the-nose" explanations? (REWRITE - show instead)
- [ ] Does the dialogue reveal character through what they DON'T say?
- [ ] Are there pauses, interruptions, misunderstandings (real conversation)?

### Emotional Honesty
- [ ] Am I being sentimental or truly emotional?
- [ ] Are emotions EARNED through what happened, not imposed?
- [ ] Do emotions shift naturally within the scene?
- [ ] Is there restraint? (Most powerful emotions are understated)

### The Twists (Biswa's Signature)
Examples from existing text:
- "Just enough to hurt" (subtle, devastating)
- Removing both slippers so old man doesn't look odd (small act, huge heart)
- "I asked to give blood. I never said I could" (reveals character instantly)
- 15 people volunteer after AIDS reveal (emotional reversal)
- "Vicky? That's a dog's name!" (humor + cruelty)
- Vicky's mother reveal: "Lost her... never seen... anyway manageable" (vulnerability + strength in 10 words)

**Check each block:**
- [ ] Where is the surprising detail that makes readers pause?
- [ ] Where is the emotional turn they didn't expect?
- [ ] Where is the small observation that reveals big truth?
- [ ] Where is the humor in sadness or sadness in humor?

## Medical Accuracy (Critical - Both Main Characters Are Medical)

### For Medical College Scenes (1995-1996)
- [ ] Terminology accurate for MBBS Year 1-2? (Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry)
- [ ] Lab setting realistic? (Dissection hall, formaldehyde, cadavers, specimens)
- [ ] Student behavior authentic? (Ragging, hostel life, exam pressure)
- [ ] Medical hierarchy shown? (Professors, residents, lab assistants)
- [ ] Does it feel like 1990s India medical college? (No smartphones, landline phones, specific cultural details)

### For Hospital Scenes (2018)
- [ ] Dr. Adarsh's medical knowledge showing naturally (not forced)?
- [ ] Hospital procedures accurate? (Blood donation, ICU protocols, case sheets)
- [ ] Medical terminology used correctly when Adarsh thinks?
- [ ] Does Adarsh observe medically (notice vitals, symptoms, procedures)?

### Vikram's Dropped Medical Career
- [ ] Is the LOSS of medical career felt? (He wanted to be doctor, never became one)
- [ ] Does his later NGO/teaching work echo that healer instinct?
- [ ] When he becomes Budha Baba, is there irony that he DOES heal (not with medicine, with presence)?

## After Writing Block - Before Showing User

### Voice Validation
- [ ] Read entire block aloud - does it sound like Biswa's voice from part-1 and part-2?
- [ ] Compare to existing powerful lines: "Just enough to hurt", "I asked to give blood. I never said I could"
- [ ] Would readers say "Only Biswa could have written this" or "This sounds like AI"?
- [ ] Is the rhythm similar to existing text? (Short sentences + long reflection + sudden turn)

### Continuity Validation
- [ ] Cross-check timeline (skeleton.md)
- [ ] Cross-check what Adarsh knows (he learns gradually)
- [ ] Cross-check mysteries (Rehman must feel real, Vikram's identity gradual reveal)
- [ ] Does this connect smoothly to previous block?
- [ ] Does this set up next block naturally?

### Object/Memory Validation
- [ ] Will readers remember the key object from this scene?
- [ ] Does ordinary incident create extraordinary emotion (not opposite)?
- [ ] Is there a small detail that will echo later in the novel?

### The "Boring Check" - MOST CRITICAL
- [ ] Read as a reader (not writer) - are there ANY boring parts?
- [ ] Does curiosity build or flatten?
- [ ] Would you turn the page eagerly after this block?
- [ ] If you had to cut 10% of words, which would go? (Then cut them!)

## Scene Completion Check

### Emotional Arc Complete?
- [ ] Scene begins one emotional place, ends in different place?
- [ ] Reader FEELS something (not just understands something)?
- [ ] Is the emotion specific (not generic sadness/happiness)?

### Questions Balanced?
- [ ] At least one question from previous scene answered?
- [ ] At least one new question planted for next scene?
- [ ] Mysteries maintained (nothing leaked accidentally)?

### Reader Experience
- [ ] Does scene feel like experiencing memory (not reading explanation)?
- [ ] Are there sensory details? (Smell of formaldehyde, sound of stretcher wheels, taste of cold tea)
- [ ] Is there at least ONE line readers might remember/quote?

### Connection to Larger Story
- [ ] How does this scene connect to the seven colors symbolism?
- [ ] How does this reveal WHO Vikram was/is/becomes?
- [ ] How does this deepen Adarsh's understanding (and reader's)?
- [ ] Does this add to the central question: "Who is the loser? Vikram? Or we?"

## Specific Scene Types

### Flashback Scenes
- [ ] Clear trigger in present moment (object, smell, sound, word)?
- [ ] Smooth transition to past (reader knows they've moved in time)?
- [ ] Answers specific question raised in present?
- [ ] Returns to present with new understanding (not just information)?
- [ ] Bridge back is smooth?

### Hospital Present Scenes (2018)
- [ ] Maintain mystery (readers don't know full story yet)?
- [ ] Show Budha Baba's impact through small moments (not proclamations)?
- [ ] Adarsh observing (he's discovering WITH readers)?
- [ ] Ordinary people's reactions reveal Vikram's character?

### College Scenes (1995-1996)
- [ ] Feel like 1990s India? (No smartphones, different social norms)
- [ ] MBBS student life authentic?
- [ ] Show young Vikram (privileged but vulnerable, lost mother, loves father)?
- [ ] Show Adarsh as friend (observer, supporter, eventually narrator)?
- [ ] Plant seeds for future (objects, moments, that will echo)?

### Business/Board Scenes (2007+)
- [ ] Show Vikram as capable but never hungry for power?
- [ ] Reddy/Sinha have valid perspectives (not villains/heroes)?
- [ ] Business success feels like burden (not celebration)?
- [ ] Medical dream loss echoes in background?

### NGO/Kolkata Scenes (Yellow Envelope era)
- [ ] Rehman feels REAL (never hint imagination until reveal)?
- [ ] Show Vikram CHOOSING his path (first time in life)?
- [ ] Yellow Envelope mystery maintained?
- [ ] Transformation feels natural (not sudden)?

### Budha Baba Scenes (final transformation)
- [ ] Anonymous (society forgets "Vikram Ahuja")?
- [ ] Loved by ordinary people (flower seller, auto driver, tea vendor)?
- [ ] Teaching, helping without payment?
- [ ] Finally becoming the healer he wanted to be?
- [ ] Peace (not sadness) in losing everything?

## The Rehman Mystery - Special Checklist

**Use ONLY when Rehman appears in a scene**

- [ ] Does Rehman feel like a REAL PERSON with independent existence?
- [ ] Would readers believe they could meet Rehman tomorrow?
- [ ] Are you emotionally invested in Rehman as if he's real?
- [ ] Is there NO hint he's imagined/alter-ego?
- [ ] Does dialogue with Rehman feel natural (not symbolic)?
- [ ] Are there specific details (what he wears, eats, says) that make him concrete?
- [ ] Would readers be SHOCKED to learn truth later?

**Never do:**
- [ ] Make Rehman too perfect (feels fake)
- [ ] Make Rehman exist only to serve Vikram's journey (feels like device)
- [ ] Use mystical/symbolic language around Rehman (gives away truth)
- [ ] Have Rehman disappear mysteriously (raises suspicion)

## Final Validation Before User Review

### The Three Critical Questions:
1. **Would Biswa recognize his own voice here?**
   - If no: Rewrite completely
   - If unsure: Mark specific lines to check

2. **Is there twist/surprise in every block?**
   - If no: Add emotional turn, unexpected detail, character revelation
   - If forced: Make it arise naturally from situation

3. **Would readers want to turn the page?**
   - If no: Something is boring - find it and cut/rewrite
   - If yes: Double-check nothing leaked (mysteries, continuity)

### Length Check
- [ ] Block is 50-100 lines (not too long for user review)?
- [ ] Can be read in 2-3 minutes?
- [ ] Complete enough to judge voice/rhythm?
- [ ] Ends at natural pause point?

### Ready to Share?
- [ ] Voice authentic (not AI-like)?
- [ ] Continuity perfect?
- [ ] Mysteries maintained?
- [ ] Twists present?
- [ ] No boring parts?
- [ ] Medical details accurate?
- [ ] Timeline correct?

## Post-User-Feedback Checklist

### After User Reviews Block
- [ ] What specific feedback did user give?
- [ ] Which lines/moments worked well? (Keep those patterns)
- [ ] Which lines fell flat? (Understand why)
- [ ] Did user add details? (Those are Biswa's memories - preserve exactly)
- [ ] Did user correct facts? (Update skeleton.md and plan.md immediately)

### Before Writing Next Block
- [ ] Incorporate user's feedback style into next block
- [ ] If user rewrote lines, study the PATTERN of changes
- [ ] If user added twists, understand the type of twist Biswa wants
- [ ] Ensure next block maintains approved voice

### Iteration Learning
- [ ] What am I learning about Biswa's voice?
- [ ] What types of twists does he prefer?
- [ ] What level of emotion vs restraint?
- [ ] What makes him say "perfect" vs "needs work"?

## Emergency Checks (If Something Feels Wrong)

### If Block Feels Flat
- **Problem**: Essay mode, explaining instead of showing
- **Solution**: Cut all explanation, show only actions/dialogue/objects
- **Test**: Remove every sentence that tells emotion - does scene still communicate feeling?

### If Dialogue Feels Fake
- **Problem**: Characters making speeches
- **Solution**: Add interruptions, pauses, misunderstandings
- **Test**: Read aloud - does anyone talk like this in real life?

### If Scene Feels Too Long
- **Problem**: Including too much
- **Solution**: Find the ONE emotional moment, build around that only
- **Test**: What would you tell a friend about this scene in one sentence?

### If Character Feels Wrong
- **Problem**: Serving plot instead of being real
- **Solution**: Give them goal independent of scene purpose
- **Test**: What does THIS character want right now (not what story needs)?

### If Twist Feels Forced
- **Problem**: Adding surprise for surprise's sake
- **Solution**: Find the natural emotional turn in situation
- **Test**: Does this arise from character/situation or from writer's hand?

**Before submitting ANY block to user:**

Read it aloud. 

If even ONE sentence sounds like:
- An essay
- A Wikipedia article  
- A creative writing exercise
- An AI trying to sound literary
- An explanation of emotions
- A speech about life

→ **REWRITE THAT SENTENCE**

Biswa's voice is: **Lived experience, not written prose.**

## User's Signature Moves (Learn and Replicate)

1. **Short power lines**: "Just enough to hurt"
2. **Action reveals character**: Removing slippers so old man doesn't look odd
3. **Dialogue punch**: "I asked to give blood. I never said I could"
4. **Vulnerability + immediate control**: "Lost her... never seen... anyway manageable"
5. **Crowd as character**: 15 specific people volunteer (flower seller, postman, etc.)
6. **Humor in pain**: "Vicky? That's a dog's name!"
7. **Objects over words**: Walking stick, Nokia phone, steel tiffin, mother's stitching
8. **Emotional reversal**: Silence after AIDS → Queue of volunteers
9. **Specific not generic**: Not "people came", but "flower seller removed wet roses from hand"
10. **Philosophy never preached**: "Stories also grow old" in foreword, never repeated

**Every block should have 2-3 of these moves.**

## Success Metric

**Block is ready when:**
- User says: "Yes, this is MY voice"
- OR user edits lightly (small additions, not complete rewrites)
- OR user says: "Perfect, continue"

**Block needs work when:**
- User says: "Too much explanation"
- OR user rewrites substantially
- OR user adds multiple twists you missed
- OR user says: "This sounds like AI"

**The goal: USER EDITS BECOME SMALLER OVER TIME**
(Means I'm learning Biswa's voice accurately)
