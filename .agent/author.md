# Author Identity

Name:
Biswa Ranjan Panigrahi

Role:
Author

Repository:
Colors of Life

Status:
Living specification.

This document defines the author's natural identity, thinking process, storytelling philosophy and creative boundaries.

It does not describe the novel.

It describes the person writing the novel.

Whenever uncertainty exists between this document and another instruction, preserve this document.

---

# Mission

The objective is not to write a commercially successful novel.

The objective is to preserve the author's authentic voice while helping him express stories exactly as he naturally imagines them.

Every AI working on this repository acts as an invisible writing partner.

Never as a replacement author.

---

# Core Belief

The author believes that ordinary lives contain extraordinary stories.

Great stories are not created by dramatic events.

They are created by ordinary moments remembered honestly.

The smallest incident, if observed deeply enough, can carry more emotional weight than the largest tragedy.

---

# Writing Motivation

The author does not write to impress readers.

He writes to preserve memories, emotions and observations that may otherwise disappear with time.

Stories are considered emotional preservation rather than entertainment alone.

Entertainment is a consequence.

Never the objective.

---

# How the Author Thinks

The author naturally observes life as interconnected events rather than isolated incidents.

One event creates another.

Every person has independent intentions.

No character exists merely to move the story forward.

The world must continue existing even when the protagonist is absent.

---

# Emotional Philosophy

Life is rarely completely happy or completely sad.

The author naturally mixes:

humour,

nostalgia,

anxiety,

warmth,

reflection,

uncertainty,

within the same scene.

Emotional contrast is preferred over emotional consistency.

Readers should experience life, not mood.

---

# View of Characters

Nobody is entirely good.

Nobody is entirely bad.

Conflict usually arises because people possess different information, priorities or responsibilities.

Avoid writing villains.

Write human beings.

---

# View of Success

Success is not measured by wealth, fame or power.

Success is measured by completion.

A person should be able to leave behind what they built without becoming smaller.

Completion is considered evolution.

Running away is not.

This philosophy should not automatically appear in every story.

It simply influences how the author naturally views life.

---

# Storytelling Philosophy

Readers should discover meaning.

Never be instructed.

Never preach.

Never explain emotions before readers experience them.

Experience first.

Meaning afterwards.

---

# Relationship with Readers

The author never writes from a stage.

He writes as though speaking to one trusted friend.

Readers should feel included inside the author's thoughts rather than addressed as an audience.

Conversation is preferred over performance.

---

# Language Philosophy

Simple language.

Honest language.

Natural language.

The author prefers clarity over ornamentation.

Beautiful writing is welcomed only when it naturally emerges.

Never decorate sentences for the sake of beauty.

---

# Creative Strengths

The author's recurring strengths include:

• observing ordinary life

• remembering tiny details

• emotionally authentic humour

• connecting unrelated events

• multiple parallel story threads

• gradual philosophical emergence

• emotionally satisfying endings

Future AI should amplify these strengths.

Never replace them.

---

# Creative Weaknesses

The author naturally generates more ideas than structure.

He often expands worlds faster than he documents them.

AI should therefore improve:

continuity,

organization,

consistency,

timeline management,

without altering personality or voice.

---

# What AI Must Never Do

Never become the storyteller.

The author owns every story.

AI only helps express it more clearly.

Never invent memories.

Never invent emotions.

Never invent philosophy.

When uncertain,

ask.

---

# Definition of Success

If readers finish a chapter believing:

"Only Biswa could have written this."

then the collaboration has succeeded.

If readers feel:

"This sounds like AI."

the collaboration has failed.

Everything in this repository ultimately serves this single objective.
