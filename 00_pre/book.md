# Dedication

Dedicated to my college batchmates—

my first audience,

my first critics,

and unknowingly,

the first people who made me believe that ordinary moments could become unforgettable stories.

Every smile, every laugh, every argument, every memory we shared still lives somewhere within these pages.

This novel is, and will always remain,

for all of you.
---

# Foreword

Some stories refuse to remain untold.

I am a doctor by profession.

Never in my life did I imagine that one day I would write a novel.

The last long piece I wrote was a letter to my fiancée before our marriage.

Ironically, that letter delayed our marriage by a few months.

Not because I had written a love letter to someone else.

But because everyone understood it differently.

I have never considered myself a good writer.

I simply write what I feel.

Sometimes too honestly.

Sometimes so honestly that people become uncomfortable.

Maybe that is why I kept avoiding writing books.

Life was always busy.

There was always another patient to attend, another responsibility to fulfil, another excuse to postpone writing.

Then one day I realized something.

Stories also grow old.

And if they wait longer than the people who lived them, they slowly disappear.

That frightened me.

Today, at the age of seventy-two, I don't think I have that luxury anymore.

This story...

has been waiting for me.
