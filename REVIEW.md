## OVERALL VERDICT

The novel is in very good shape. The bones are strong — the story moves with genuine weight, the objects (pathology book, brown chair, three-legged horse, yellow envelope) do real work, and the twist architecture is now properly planted. The prose has improved significantly from earlier drafts.

**What still needs work:**
- 8 places where the narration editorializes instead of trusting the scene. These are AI-tell lines. Real Indian authors trust the scene.
- 3 scenes emotionally undercooked — right ingredients, stop one beat too early.
- 1 duplicate line (Ch1/Part-2) from the editing session that was not cleaned up.
- Rehman's terrace scene: one line gives away his fictional status inconsistently.
